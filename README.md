## Overview

This project explores how **education, occupation, and workplace factors** influence:

- Income levels  
- Employment outcomes  
- Employee satisfaction  

The analysis is based on multiple datasets and statistical methods to identify key patterns in career outcomes.

---

## Research Question

How do education, employment characteristics, and workplace factors influence income, employment outcomes, and employee satisfaction?

---

## Datasets Used

- Graduate employment outcomes (education vs salary and employability)
- MOM employment statistics (income by occupation, gender, age, qualification)
- Glassdoor employee reviews (workplace satisfaction and sentiment)

---

## Methodology

- Descriptive statistical analysis  
- Comparative analysis across groups  
- Income trend analysis by age, gender, and qualification  
- Sentiment-based evaluation of workplace reviews  
- Visualization and interpretation using Python  

---

## Key Findings

- Education strongly influences starting salary and employability  
- Professional and technical fields show the highest career stability and income  
- Income increases with qualification level but gaps widen over time  
- Gender wage gaps are more significant in high-income occupations  
- Workplace environment factors directly affect employee satisfaction and retention
